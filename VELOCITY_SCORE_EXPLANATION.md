# Velocity Score Explanation

## What is Velocity?

**Velocity** in reselling refers to **how quickly an item sells** - essentially, the speed at which inventory turns over. It's a critical metric for resellers because:

- **Faster velocity** = Quicker return on investment = More capital turnover
- **Slower velocity** = Tied-up capital = Less flexibility

## How Velocity is Assessed

The AI analyzes multiple factors to determine velocity:

1. **Recent Sold Listings:** How many items sold recently on eBay/Amazon
2. **Time to Sale:** How quickly listings convert to sales
3. **Market Demand Indicators:** Search volume, view counts, watchers
4. **Category Popularity:** How active the category is
5. **Brand Recognition:** Well-known brands typically move faster
6. **Trending Status:** Current market trends and seasonal factors

## Why Velocity Matters

### For Resellers:
- **Capital Efficiency:** High velocity items let you reinvest profits faster
- **Risk Management:** Fast-moving items reduce risk of price drops
- **Scalability:** Can process more items with same capital
- **Cash Flow:** Faster sales = better cash flow

### Example Scenario:
- **High Velocity Item:** $10 buy ‚Üí $50 sell in 1 week = $40 profit, repeat 4x/month = $160/month
- **Low Velocity Item:** $10 buy ‚Üí $50 sell in 3 months = $40 profit, only 1x/quarter = $40/quarter

Same profit per item, but **12x more profit per month** with high velocity!

## Balancing Velocity and Profit

The best reselling strategy often balances:
- **High Velocity + High Profit** = Best case (rare, jump on these!)
- **High Velocity + Low Profit** = Still good for volume
- **Low Velocity + High Profit** = Acceptable if profit margin is very high
- **Low Velocity + Low Profit** = Avoid (not worth the capital tie-up)
